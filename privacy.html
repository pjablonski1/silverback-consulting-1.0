Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how Silverback Consulting LLC collects, uses, stores, and protects information submitted through this website, the Silverback Client Portal, scheduling forms, investor intake forms, email notifications, and related demo workflows.

This page is a business-ready template and should be reviewed by a qualified attorney before public launch.

Information We May Collect

We may collect information you provide directly, including your name, email address, phone number, company name, business type, investment interest, scheduling details, messages, documents, invoice information, payment preferences, and information about your business goals or challenges.

If you use the client portal, we may collect portal activity such as login events, messages, document review status, simulated e-signature status, invoice views, payment submissions, and audit log activity.

Payment, Banking, And Document Information

The current website may demonstrate payment, DocuSign-style e-signature, and Plaid-style bank connection workflows. In production, payments should be processed through secure third-party providers such as Stripe, PayPal, Venmo, Apple Pay, Google Pay, ACH processors, and Plaid. Silverback should not store full payment card numbers, banking credentials, or sensitive authentication credentials in the browser.

DocuSign, Plaid, payment processors, and hosting providers may have their own privacy policies that apply when those services are connected.

How We Use Information

Information may be used to respond to inquiries, schedule consultations, evaluate business needs, manage investor or client intake, prepare recommendations, provide client portal access, maintain CRM records, send notifications, generate invoices or statements, process payments, and improve Silverback's services.

Email Notifications

Website and portal actions may prepare or send notifications to Silverback team members, including appointment requests, investor intake submissions, client portal messages, signed document notices, payment notices, and related CRM activity.

Data Storage And Security

The current demo may use browser localStorage to simulate CRM and portal records. Production deployment should use secure backend storage, HTTPS, encrypted databases, access controls, audit logs, secure file storage, and role-based permissions. Production login should use a secure identity provider such as Azure AD B2C or a comparable authentication system with multi-factor authentication.

Sharing Information

Silverback may share information with trusted service providers that help operate the website, portal, CRM, email notifications, scheduling, payment processing, document signing, bank verification, analytics, hosting, or customer support. Silverback should not sell personal information.

Your Choices

You may request access, correction, or deletion of information you provided by contacting Silverback. Certain records may be retained when needed for business, legal, accounting, security, or compliance purposes.
